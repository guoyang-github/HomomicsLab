# Funding Mechanisms and Program Types

## Overview

Understanding the various funding mechanisms and program types helps you identify the best match for your research stage, goals, and resources. This reference catalogs major mechanisms by agency.

## National Science Foundation (NSF)

### Research Grants
- **Standard Grants**: $100K-$500K, 3-5 years, single PI or small team
- **Collaborative Research**: Multiple institutions, separately submitted
- **Continuing Grants**: Multi-year funding with annual increments

### Career Development
- **CAREER**: Junior faculty, $400-600K over 5 years, integrated research/education
- **Research Initiation Awards**: For institutions with limited NSF funding

### Rapid Response and Exploratory
- **RAPID**: Urgent research, up to $200K, 1 year, no preliminary data
- **EAGER**: High-risk exploratory, up to $300K, 2 years
- **RUI**: Research at Undergraduate Institutions

### Centers and Large Programs
- **Science and Technology Centers (STC)**: $5M/year, 5 years
- **Engineering Research Centers (ERC)**: Similar scale, industry partnership
- **Materials Research Science and Engineering Centers (MRSEC)**
- **Industry/University Cooperative Research Centers (I/UCRC)**

## National Institutes of Health (NIH)

### Research Project Grants (R Series)
- **R01**: Primary research grant, $250K+/year, 3-5 years
- **R21**: Exploratory/developmental, $275K over 2 years, no preliminary data
- **R03**: Small grant, $100K over 2 years, pilot data
- **R15**: AREA grants for undergraduate institutions
- **R35**: MIRA, program-specific multi-project support

### Training and Career Development (K Series)
- **K01**: Mentored Research Scientist Development
- **K08**: Mentored Clinical Scientist
- **K99/R00**: Pathway to Independence

### Fellowship Awards (F Series)
- **F31**: Predoctoral fellowship
- **F32**: Postdoctoral fellowship
- **F99/K00**: Predoctoral to Postdoctoral Transition

### Program Project Grants
- **P01**: Multi-project integrated research programs
- **P41**: Biotechnology Resource Grants
- **U01**: Cooperative agreements with substantial NIH involvement

## Department of Energy (DOE)

### Office of Science
- **Single Investigator Grants**: Basic research in physical, biological, computing sciences
- **Energy Frontier Research Centers (EFRC)**: $2-4M/year team grants
- **SciDAC**: Scientific Discovery through Advanced Computing

### ARPA-E (Advanced Research Projects Agency-Energy)
- **OPEN Programs**: Broad solicitations for transformative energy tech
- **FOCUS Programs**: Targeted technology areas
- **Teaming partnerships** often required
- **Cost sharing** typically required

### EERE (Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy)
- Applied research and development programs
- Industry partnership emphasis
- Technology commercialization pathways required

## DARPA (Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency)

### Program Structure
- **Broad Agency Announcements (BAAs)**: Program-specific solicitations
- **Direct to Phase II**: For technologies with prior development
- **Young Faculty Award (YFA)**: Early career, up to $500K
- **Director's Fellowship**: High-risk paradigm-shifting research

### Technical Offices
- **BTO**: Biological Technologies
- **DSO**: Defense Sciences
- **I2O**: Information Innovation
- **MTO**: Microsystems Technology
- **STO**: Strategic Technology
- **TTO**: Tactical Technology

## Foundation and Private Funding

### Major Private Foundations
- **Simons Foundation**: Mathematics, physics, biology, autism
- **Gates Foundation**: Global health, development, education
- **Howard Hughes Medical Institute**: Investigator programs
- **Alfred P. Sloan Foundation**: Research and education
- **Pew Charitable Trusts**: Biomedical research
- **March of Dimes**: Pregnancy and infant health
- **American Heart Association**: Cardiovascular research

### Corporate Funding
- Industry-sponsored research agreements
- Cooperative Research and Development Agreements (CRADAs)
- Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) / Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR)

## Choosing the Right Mechanism

### Decision Matrix

| Factor | Early Career | Mid-Career | Established | Team/ Center |
|--------|-------------|------------|-------------|--------------|
| **NSF** | CAREER | Standard Grant | Large Grants | STC, ERC |
| **NIH** | K99/R00, F32 | R01 | Multiple R01s | P01, U01 |
| **DOE** | Early Career | Single Investigator | Program Lead | EFRC |
| **DARPA** | YFA | Program Teams | Program Directors | BAAs |

### Key Considerations
1. **Career stage**: Match mechanism to experience level
2. **Research stage**: Preliminary data requirements vary
3. **Team size**: Some mechanisms require collaboration
4. **Budget needs**: Ensure mechanism can support proposed work
5. **Timeline**: Some are annual cycles, some are rolling
6. **Resubmission policies**: Understand chances for revision
