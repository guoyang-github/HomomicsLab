# Budget Preparation and Justification

## Overview

A well-prepared budget demonstrates that you understand the true costs of the proposed research and have allocated resources appropriately. The budget justification is your opportunity to explain why each expense is necessary for project success.

## Budget Categories

### Personnel
- **PI and Co-I Salary**: Base salary multiplied by percent effort
- **Postdoctoral Researchers**: Salary plus fringe benefits
- **Graduate Students**: Stipend, tuition, and benefits
- **Technical Staff**: Research assistants and technicians
- **Administrative Support**: When specifically required by project

**Key Principles**:
- Percent effort must be consistent with narrative description
- NIH salary cap applies (~$221,900 for 2024)
- NSF allows up to 2 months summer salary for faculty
- Include annual cost-of-living adjustments (typically 3%)

### Equipment
- Definition: Items costing $5,000+ with useful life >1 year (most agencies)
- Justify why existing equipment is insufficient
- Include maintenance and service contracts
- Note: Some agencies limit equipment purchases

### Travel
- **Professional Meetings**: Conference registration, airfare, lodging
- **Collaborative Visits**: Travel to partner institutions
- **Fieldwork Travel**: Site visits and data collection
- **Program Meetings**: Required agency meetings (especially DARPA)

**Typical Limits**:
- NSF: $10,000/year for domestic conferences
- NIH: $2,500/person per meeting typical
- International travel requires additional justification

### Materials and Supplies
- Consumables and reagents
- Software licenses and subscriptions
- Publication costs (increasingly important)
- Participant incentives for human subjects research

### Other Direct Costs
- Subawards to collaborating institutions
- Consultant fees (with rates and hours justified)
- Equipment maintenance and service contracts
- Tuition remission for graduate students

### Indirect Costs (F&A)
- Negotiated rate with institution
- Applied to Modified Total Direct Costs (MTDC)
- Some items excluded from MTDC base (equipment, tuition, participant costs)
- Rate varies by institution (typically 40-60% for research universities)

## Agency-Specific Budget Formats

### NIH Modular Budget
- Used for R01 requests <=$250,000 direct costs per year
- Budget in $25,000 modules
- Minimal detail required in application
- Detailed budget kept on file at institution

### NIH Detailed Budget
- Required for >$250,000/year or complex mechanisms
- Itemized by category with justifications
- Consortium/contractual costs broken out
- Budget justification limited to specific page limits

### NSF Budget
- Standard budget table format
- Senior personnel salary capped at 2 months
- No indirect cost rate cap
- Subawards require separate budget

### DOE Budget
- Often requires cost sharing
- Quarterly or annual budget breakdowns
- National lab collaborations budgeted separately
- Equipment purchases may require additional documentation

### DARPA Budget
- Phase-based budgeting
- Supporting cost data for large procurements
- Cost realism analysis
- Travel for program meetings typically required

## Budget Justification Writing

### Personnel Justification
- Explain percent effort for each individual
- Describe specific responsibilities
- Show consistency with narrative timeline
- Address salary cap limitations

### Equipment Justification
- Explain why existing equipment cannot be used
- Describe how equipment will support research
- Include quotes or estimates when possible
- Note sharing plans if equipment will be shared

### Travel Justification
- List specific conferences or meetings
- Explain purpose of each trip
- Show cost estimates based on current rates
- Limit to essential travel

## Common Budget Mistakes

1. **Inconsistency**: Budget doesn't match narrative description
2. **Over/Under Budgeting**: Asking for too much or too little
3. **Missing Categories**: Forgetting essential costs
4. **Vague Justifications**: "Needed for research" is insufficient
5. **Math Errors**: Incorrect calculations or percentages
6. **Policy Violations**: Exceeding salary caps or effort limits
7. **Missing Inflation**: Not including annual increases
