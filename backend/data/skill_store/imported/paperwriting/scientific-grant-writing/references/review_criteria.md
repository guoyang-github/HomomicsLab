# Detailed Review Criteria by Funding Agency

## Overview

Understanding how your proposal will be evaluated is essential for writing a competitive application. This reference provides detailed review criteria for major U.S. funding agencies.

## NSF Merit Review Criteria

### Intellectual Merit (Primary Criterion)
Reviewers evaluate:

1. **What is the potential for the proposed activity to advance knowledge?**
   - Does the project address important questions?
   - Will the results advance the field significantly?
   - Is the proposed work creative and original?

2. **How well-conceived and organized is the proposed activity?**
   - Are the research plan and methods sound?
   - Is the timeline realistic?
   - Are potential problems addressed?

3. **How well-qualified is the individual or team?**
   - Does the team have appropriate expertise?
   - Is there evidence of prior success?
   - Are institutional resources adequate?

4. **Is there sufficient access to resources?**
   - Are facilities and equipment available?
   - Is the institutional environment supportive?
   - Are collaborations well-established?

### Broader Impacts (Equally Important)
Reviewers evaluate:

1. **Potential to benefit society**
   - Will the research have practical applications?
   - Does it address societal needs?
   - Will it contribute to economic competitiveness?

2. **Meaningful engagement with broader impacts**
   - Are broader impacts integrated into the research plan?
   - Are activities specific and well-planned?
   - How will impacts be measured?

### Additional Review Considerations
- Results from prior NSF support (if applicable)
- Integration of research and education
- Diversity and inclusion efforts
- Data management and sharing plans

## NIH Scored Review Criteria (1-9 Scale)

### 1. Significance
- Does the project address an important problem?
- Will scientific knowledge be advanced?
- Will clinical practice or public health be improved?
- Is there a compelling rationale?

**Scoring**: 1 = exceptionally significant; 9 = not significant

### 2. Investigator(s)
- Are the investigators well-suited to the project?
- Do they have appropriate training and expertise?
- Is there evidence of productivity and impact?

**Scoring**: 1 = exceptionally suited; 9 = poorly suited

### 3. Innovation
- Are the concepts or approaches novel?
- Does the project challenge existing paradigms?
- Are the methods innovative?

**Scoring**: 1 = exceptionally innovative; 9 = not innovative

### 4. Approach
- Are the methods well-reasoned and appropriate?
- Are potential problems and alternatives addressed?
- Is the project feasible within the timeline?
- Are statistical methods adequate?

**Scoring**: 1 = exceptionally rigorous; 9 = seriously flawed

### 5. Environment
- Are institutional resources adequate?
- Does the scientific environment support success?
- Are collaborative arrangements appropriate?

**Scoring**: 1 = exceptionally supportive; 9 = inadequate

### Additional Review Considerations (Not Scored)
- Human subjects protections
- Inclusion of women, minorities, and children
- Vertebrate animal welfare
- Biohazards and safety
- Budget and period appropriateness
- Resubmission changes (if applicable)

### Overall Impact
The overall impact score synthesizes all criteria into a single assessment of the project's likelihood to exert a sustained, powerful influence on the research field. Lower scores are better (1-9 scale).

## DOE Review Criteria

Varies by program office, but generally includes:

### Scientific/Technical Merit
- Quality and originality of research approach
- Technical feasibility and risk assessment
- Relevance to DOE mission and program goals
- Potential impact on the field

### Qualifications and Resources
- Investigator expertise and track record
- Adequacy of facilities and equipment
- Institutional support and commitment

### Management and Budget
- Realism of proposed timeline
- Appropriateness of budget
- Quality of project management plan

## DARPA Review Criteria

### Technical Merit
- Novelty and innovation of proposed approach
- Technical feasibility and risk
- Potential for transformative impact
- Soundness of methodology

### Mission Relevance
- Contribution to DARPA mission
- Relevance to stated program goals
- Potential for national security benefit

### Transition and Impact
- Plans for technology transition
- Commercialization or operational deployment potential
- Metrics for success

### Team and Management
- Team qualifications and experience
- Project management approach
- Realism of schedule and budget

## Writing for Reviewers

### Strategy by Criterion
- **Significance**: Open with compelling problem statement
- **Innovation**: Clearly state what is new, don't assume it's obvious
- **Approach**: Provide enough detail for feasibility assessment
- **Investigator**: Highlight relevant accomplishments
- **Environment**: Describe unique institutional resources

### Common Reviewer Concerns
- Overly ambitious scope for timeline/budget
- Weak preliminary data for high-risk approaches
- Methods insufficiently described
- Team lacks critical expertise
- Significance not adequately justified
- Innovation claims not supported by literature review
