# Research Methods Guidance for Grant Proposals

## Overview

The research methods section must convince reviewers that your approach is rigorous, feasible, and appropriate for the questions being asked. This reference provides discipline-specific guidance for crafting compelling methodology narratives.

## Universal Principles

### Reproducibility and Rigor
- Describe methods in sufficient detail for independent replication
- Include sample sizes, power analyses, and statistical plans
- Address potential confounders and control strategies
- Specify data management and sharing plans
- Reference established protocols and standards

### Feasibility Demonstration
- Reference preliminary data showing methods work
- Cite publications using similar approaches
- Describe pilot studies or proof-of-concept results
- Acknowledge limitations and provide alternatives
- Show timeline is realistic for proposed methods

## Experimental Research

### Design Elements
- **Controls**: Positive, negative, and appropriate experimental controls
- **Randomization**: How samples/ subjects will be randomized
- **Blinding**: Procedures for blinding investigators when possible
- **Replication**: Biological vs. technical replicates, sample sizes
- **Validation**: Independent validation cohorts or cross-validation

### Statistical Analysis Plan
- Primary and secondary endpoints clearly defined
- Statistical tests appropriate for data type and distribution
- Correction for multiple comparisons
- Handling of missing data and outliers
- Software and version numbers for analysis tools

## Computational Research

### Algorithm Development
- Clear problem formulation and mathematical framework
- Benchmark datasets and evaluation metrics
- Comparison with existing methods
- Computational complexity and scalability analysis
- Open-source code availability plans

### Data Science Approaches
- Data sources, quality, and preprocessing steps
- Feature engineering and selection strategies
- Model validation (cross-validation, holdout sets)
- Interpretability and explainability methods
- Ethical considerations and bias mitigation

## Clinical and Translational Research

### Study Design
- Phase-appropriate design (I, II, III, IV)
- Inclusion/exclusion criteria justification
- Recruitment strategies and timelines
- Primary and secondary outcome measures
- Safety monitoring and stopping rules

### Regulatory Considerations
- IRB approval status or plans
- IND/IDE requirements if applicable
- Data and Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB)
- Adverse event reporting procedures
- Clinical trial registration (ClinicalTrials.gov)

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Insufficient detail**: Methods too vague for feasibility assessment
2. **Overly ambitious**: Proposing techniques beyond team's expertise
3. **No alternatives**: Failing to describe backup plans
4. **Weak statistics**: Inadequate power or inappropriate tests
5. **Ignoring limitations**: Not addressing potential problems proactively
